Bali, Indonesia

Island of Gods

Overview

Bali is an Indonesian island known for its forested volcanic mountains, iconic rice paddies, beaches and coral reefs. The island is home to religious sites such as cliffside Uluwatu Temple. To the south, the beachside city of Kuta has lively bars, while Seminyak, Sanur and Nusa Dua are popular resort towns.

The island's cultural heart is Ubud, home to the traditional crafts and dance. The ancient holy site Tirta Empul Temple features ceremonial pools where locals and visitors alike go for ritual purification.

Top Attractions

  • Uluwatu Temple
  • Sacred Monkey Forest
  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces
  • Tanah Lot Temple
  • Ubud Palace
  • Mount Batur Sunrise Trek

Food & Drinks

Balinese cuisine is vibrant and flavorful, offering a unique culinary experience. Must-try dishes include:

  • Babi Guling - Roasted suckling pig
  • Nasi Campur - Mixed rice with various sides
  • Satay Lilit - Seafood satay
  • Lawar -Traditional meat salad

Oceanfronts resorts Nusa Dua

Ubud

Boutique hotels in Ubud and Sanur

Kutaa

Guesthouses and hostels in Kuta and Canggu

Best time to visit

The best time to visit Bali is during the dry season from April to October, with peak tourist season in July and August.

Apr - Jun Exellent
Apr - Jun Peak season
Sep - Oct Exellent
Sep - Oct Rainy Season

Getting There

Bali is served by Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) in Denpasar. Direct flights are available from major Asian hubs, Australia, and select European cities.

From the airport, you can reach most destinations by:

  • Pre-arranged hotel transfer
  • Airport taxi service
  • Ride-sharing apps (Grab, Gojek)

Practical Info

  • Language: Indonesian (Bahasa)
  • Currency: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
  • Power: Indonesian (Bahasa)
  • Internet: Indonesian (Bahasa)
